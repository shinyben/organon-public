## **What is this?**
A public version of my personal knowledge system originally built to organize my thoughts, books, and notes. The private side contains 8 years of journals, highlights from hundreds of books, and raw, unfiltered drafts. I’ve started fresh for the public version, given how many others wouldn't like Organon-private published. 

Think of it as a weaponized notebook. Or a confession with no priest. Or just a stranger’s brain, left open on the table. 

Use it however you want. 

The private version will be a gift to a select few.

## **Who is this?**
A runaway from the American suburbs - former actor, ex-football player, MIT dropout (twice: once from computer science, once from a master’s in AI). A compulsive autodidact who traded classrooms for cheap apartments in Quito and Medellín, where I taught myself Spanish and ripped apart those 250 books to feed a private obsession: building a machine to catch every thought before it escaped.

This is the spillover. The public-facing debris of a decade-long project - part intellectual scrapbook, part philosophical fever dream - assembled from whatever stuck:

- **The highbrow** (Kant, Deleuze, Marx)
- **The underground** (accelerationist manifestos, gnostic gospels, cyberpunk)
- **The unclassifiable** (Lynchian rants, Reichian orgone, linguistic warfare)

I work remotely, live out of a backpack, and spend money on two things: books and time. The rest is noise.

## **What's in here?**
A slow emergence of a sprawling, interconnected web of ideas - philosophy, politics, technology, psychology, and beyond - curated from years of obsessive reading, thinking, writing. This is not a static archive but a public layer on top of a living system, a tool for grappling with the chaos of modern thought. I will build the public layer as is needed: a tool for communicating difficult concepts to the people in my life.

You’ll find:
- **Existential dilemmas** (absurdity, alienation)
- **Power dynamics** (propaganda, surveillance)
- **Systemic critiques** (late capitalism, colonialism)
- **Mind and machine** (cybernetics, AI/ML)
- **Myth, religion, and hidden knowledge** (Gnosticism, archetypes)
- **Radical futures and dystopias** (accelerationism, posthumanism)

Each topic is a thread in a larger tapestry, pulling from thinkers like Deleuze, Jung, Marx, and Jameson, as well as outliers and iconoclasts. Some entries are polished; others are raw, half-formed probes into the unknown. The goal isn’t dogma but exploration - a way to map the fractures and contradictions of the world without collapsing into simplicity.

This is for those who want to think _dangerously_, to follow ideas wherever they lead. Dive in, pull a thread, see what unravels. Email me about it and, with permission, the discussion might end up on here. 99bengardner [@] gmail.com

## Why "Organon"
Organon (*ὄργανον*): "tool" or "instrument" in the Greek. Etymologically shared with organ, organism, get it? 
- Aristotle, *Organon*, works on logic
- Francis Bacon, *Novum Organum* (*New Tool*, Latin), proposal of scientific method
- Wilhelm Reich (psychoanalyst), Orgonon, research center[^2]
- Gilles Deleuze (philosopher), *Corps sans Organes* (*Body without Organs*, French), concept in *Capitalism and Schizophrenia*[^3]
